About

I'm a passionate computer science student with a keen interest in Programming Language research, particularly applied to Human-Computer Interaction (HCI). My journey involves advanced coursework, internships, and research experiences in HCI, Robotics, and Neuroscience.

During my previous experiences, I actively participated in projects aimed at enhancing climate services through the application of human-computer interaction theory. These endeavors involved the integration of HCI principles to improve the accessibility and effectiveness of climate-related information and services, ultimately benefitting a wider audience. Additionally, I have been deeply involved in research focused on healthcare digital twins, employing generative AI and virtual reality technologies.
